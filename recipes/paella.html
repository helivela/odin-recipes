Paella valenciana

Description

Paella is one of the most emblematic dishes of Spanish cuisine and is enjoyed in many variations across the country. It is a rice-based dish traditionally cooked in a wide, shallow pan and flavored with saffron and a variety of ingredients depending on the region. This recipe is about the most traditional and widely recognized version, Valencian paella, which is prepared with chicken, rabbit, green beans, butter beans, tomatoes, olive oil, and aromatic seasonings.

Ingredients

(5 servings)
  • 300 g of bomba rice
  • 150 g of chicken
  • 150 g of rabbit
  • 100 g of flat green beans
  • 100 g of lima beans
  • 2 grated ripe tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Saffron
  • 600 ml of water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a paella pan and add the chopped chicken and rabbit. Sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add the green beans and lima beans and cook for a few more minutes before adding the grated tomatoes. Cook until a sort of sofrito forms.
  3. Add hot water, saffron and salt to taste. Add the rice when water boils and cook for 20 minutes at medium heat.
  4. Once the rice is almost ready, turn the heat to maximum for 30 to 40 seconds then turn it off. Let it rest for a few minutes.
